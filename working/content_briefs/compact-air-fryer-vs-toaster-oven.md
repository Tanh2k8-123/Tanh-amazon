## Search intent

The reader wants a direct trade-off answer: which appliance fits their cooking style and space better?

They must know:

- Air fryers usually excel at fast convection cooking and crispy small batches.
- Toaster ovens can be more versatile for toast, reheating, and flat foods.
- Door swing, basket pull-out, crumb trays, heat clearance, and cleaning differ.
- The best choice depends on foods cooked most often.

What makes this article better:

- Compare by real small-kitchen constraints, not generic feature lists.
- Include a decision table.
- Say who should buy neither or delay the purchase.

## Comparison criteria

| Criterion | Air fryer angle | Toaster oven angle |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Footprint | Often taller/deeper basket shape | Often wider with door swing |
| Cooking style | Crispy small batches | Toast, flat foods, reheating |
| Cleaning | Basket and insert cleaning | Crumb tray and rack cleaning |
| Storage | Bulky but contained | Wide shape can be awkward |
| Best for | Frozen foods, vegetables, small proteins | Toast, leftovers, open-face foods |
